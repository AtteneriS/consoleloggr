Update: Flexbox Fun and Frustration

I mostly procrastinated, and time slipped through my hands again... surprise!

Well, it's not like I've been completely static. I've been studying on and off for the past month, but mostly it's been random, disconnected days. So, improvement, I guess? It's not real consistency, but I didn't have large stretches of days with no studying either.

So, what did I do?

I left off starting to dip my toes into Flexbox, and this month was a slow progression of that. I built a responsive nav (nothing fancy, really clunky, mostly to illustrate different screen sizes). I also learned a tiny bit about Chrome DevTools; now I know I can see padding and margins, and set the screen view from there.

And then I built a pricing panel on my own. I realized Colt goes straight into telling you what to do and explains very little about why he does what he does or his thought process. So I decided to build it on my own before the "code-along." To say it was a challenge is an understatement.

First of all, Colt used a "CSS resetter" that I decided not to use (because I'm too cool for that, obviously). So I looked up one that was fairly plain, one that I could sort of understand what it was resetting and why, and used that instead.

Then I went into actual coding, grabbed the icons from the source code, and set up the basic HTML. I set the background color and the main card color - cool, cool. First problem... how do I center this?

I know.
I know.
"How to center a div" is like the most overused programmer joke that isn't even relevant anymore because with Flexbox it's SUPER easy. Well, I didn't know how to do it. To be honest, I did think that when dealing with a card like this, you were supposed to do something similar to the box model for the card, and then for the elements inside, you'd use actual Flexbox. I don't know why, but I thought that made a lot more sense.

Anyway, card centered. What now? Make the elements sit side by side, simple. Flexbox. Now the disaster starts. Why do they overflow? Okay, I need to set a height for the images. Why do they still overflow? Oh, I forgot it's the width, not the height. Now what? Why is the div of each category not respecting the dimensions? Why isn't the div expanding? Why does 'flex-grow' do nothing? Why does 'flex-basis' do nothing here...? And a lot of that: a lot of Googling, a lot of Stack Overflow, cheatsheets, and a lot of AI asking: "Please explain this like I'm a 5-year-old foreigner who barely speaks English, better yet explain it like I'm a 5-year-old alien!"

Anyway, in the end I got it, and it was pretty similar to Colt's. So I went to see his code-along, and it turns out he does it "mobile first" and I didn't ü´† BUT I did do it responsively, just like his! That's something, isn't it? I mean, it was much simpler with mobile-first, but not that different from what I did. And (humble-bragging noob here) my full-screen CSS looks better, in my opinion.
